What is Diabetic Retinopathy(DR)?

Diabetic retinopathy (DR) is a complication of diabetes that damages the light-sensitive retina in your eye. High blood sugar weakens tiny blood vessels in the retina, causing them to leak fluid or bleed. In some cases, abnormal new blood vessels grow, further threatening your vision. Early DR often has no symptoms, but regular eye exams can detect it before vision loss occurs.

Why is Early Detection Important?

Early detection of diabetic retinopathy (DR) is crucial! DR often progresses silently, with no warning signs until vision is already at risk. However, catching DR early allows for treatment that can prevent vision loss entirely. Regular eye exams are your best defense against blindness from DR. Don't wait for symptoms - schedule your screening today!
